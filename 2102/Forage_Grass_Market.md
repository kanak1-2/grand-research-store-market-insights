"Forage Grass" refers particularly to food given to the animals (including plants cut and carried to them), rather than that which they forage for themselves (called forage). Forage Grass is also called provender and includes hay, straw, silage, compressed and pelleted feeds, oils and mixed rations, and sprouted grains and legumes (such as bean sprouts, fresh malt, or spent malt). Most animal feed is from plants, but some manufacturers add ingredients to processed feeds that are of animal origin.</p>
