Report Overview



Osteosynthesis is defined as the fixation of a bone. It is a surgical procedure to treat bone fractures in which bone fragments are joined with screws, plates, nails or wires. The external fixator is now commonly used for the treatment of open fracture, especially of the lower extremity. Multiple pins are introduced into the bone and are interconnected with external rods.
