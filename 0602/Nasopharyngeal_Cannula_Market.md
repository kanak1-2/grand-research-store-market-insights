These devices are typically made from hollow plastic or soft rubber tubes designed to fit through the nasal passages into the nasopharynx, allowing the delivery of oxygen directly to the patient???s airway. They are used in situations where other ventilation methods, such as bag-mask ventilation, are less effective, particularly in patients who have difficulties with airway management or require a less invasive approach for respiratory support.</p>
 
 
 
 <p>Nasopharyngeal cannulas are commonly used in both hospital and clinical settings for patients with conditions such as respiratory distress, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), or during emergency situations where the patient needs immediate oxygenation support. These devices are an essential part of the healthcare toolkit, providing a safe, non-invasive method for enhancing oxygenation in patients with compromised respiratory systems.</p>
