 <p>Non-invasive insulin refers to insulin delivery methods that do not require traditional subcutaneous injections. These alternatives are developed to improve patient comfort and compliance. Common forms of non-injectable insulin include inhalable insulin, such as Afrezza, which is absorbed through the lungs, and oral insulin, which is being researched to survive the digestive tract and be absorbed into the bloodstream. These methods aim to offer more convenient and less invasive options for managing diabetes, reducing the burden of frequent injections, and potentially enhancing the quality of life for patients.</p>
