These delivery systems aim to trigger both systemic and mucosal immune responses without the need for needle-based administration. Such vaccines are designed to enhance patient compliance, reduce the discomfort associated with injections, and provide a more accessible way to distribute vaccines, especially in areas where healthcare infrastructure may be limited.</p>
 
 
 
 <p>Key products in this market include oral vaccines (which are taken by mouth) and nasal spray vaccines (which are inhaled through the nose). Both methods offer potential advantages, including ease of use, reduced risk of needle-stick injuries, and the possibility of delivering vaccines in mass immunization campaigns.</p>
