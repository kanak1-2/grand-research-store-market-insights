 <p><strong>Catalyst Carriers and Binders</strong> are materials used to support and stabilize active catalytic components, enhancing their effectiveness and durability. Catalyst carriers, also known as supports, provide a porous structure that increases the surface area available for catalytic reactions, facilitating better reactant access to active sites. Common carrier materials include alumina, silica, and activated carbon.</p>
 
 
 
 <p>? </p>
 
 
 
 <p>Binders, on the other hand, are used to hold the active catalytic particles and carrier materials together, ensuring structural integrity, particularly in high-temperature or high-pressure environments. They help maintain the catalyst???s physical form while minimizing particle breakdown or sintering. Binders often include clay, silica, and alumina, among others, and are essential in applications such as petrochemical processing, emission control, and environmental catalysis.</p>
