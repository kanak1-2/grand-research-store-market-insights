<h1>Optically Variable Ink Market, Global Outlook and Forecast 2025-2032</h1><br/><p>Optically Variable Ink (OVI) is an anti-counterfeiting measure used on many major modern banknotes, as well as on Others official documents (professional licenses, for example). The trademark for OVI is owned by SICPA SA. The ink displays two distinct colors depending on the angle the bill is viewed at. For example, the United States fifty-dollar bill uses color shifting ink for the numeral 50 so that it displays copper at one angle and bright green in anOthers. OVI is based on the reflection of different wavelengths of light by the ink, which varies with the viewing angle. A color copier or scanner cannot reproduce this effect, as it can only copy a document at one fixed angle.</p>
